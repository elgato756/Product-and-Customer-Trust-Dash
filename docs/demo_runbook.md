# Demo Runbook — AI Customer Trust & Enterprise Assurance Workflow

## Demo Goal

This demo shows how a Customer Trust / GRC team can use AI-assisted workflows to intake enterprise security requests, route them by customer region and stakeholder review needs, map responses to approved evidence, track SLAs, and reduce unsupported external claims.

The workflow is inspired by real customer trust pain points:
- High-volume security questionnaires
- Repetitive customer diligence requests
- Regional differences in customer concerns
- Need for Legal, Privacy, Security, Product, Field Security, GTM, or Customer Success review
- Pressure to support deal velocity without overstating security or compliance claims

## Demo Story

A customer or GTM partner submits a security / trust request. The system classifies the request, identifies regional patterns, maps likely evidence, recommends a stakeholder review path, and flags whether the team can answer directly or needs additional review.

The goal is not to replace human review. The goal is to front-load context, reduce repetitive work, and help teams respond faster while keeping customer-facing claims accurate and supportable.

## Recommended Demo Flow

### 1. Open the dashboard

Start with the dashboard overview.

Point out:
- Total customer trust requests
- High-priority requests
- Region-routed requests
- SLA / turnaround visibility
- Requests requiring stakeholder review

Explain that this mirrors a customer trust intake queue used by GRC, Field Security, Sales, and Customer Success teams.

### 2. Show regional routing

Open a Japan / APAC request.

Explain that different regions often ask different categories of questions. For example:
- Japan / APAC customers may focus on data residency, local documentation, subprocessors, cross-border transfers, and dedicated storage patterns.
- EU / EEA customers may focus on DPA terms, SCCs, subprocessors, retention, deletion, privacy posture, and AI/data-use boundaries.
- US financial services customers may focus on SOC 2 Type 2, CAIQ, encryption, access controls, audit evidence, and dedicated control explanations.

The point is that the system can analyze historical request patterns and front-load likely evidence and review paths before the customer asks every follow-up question.

### 3. Show evidence mapping

Open a request with mapped evidence.

Point out how the system connects customer-facing answers to approved materials such as:
- SOC 2 Type 2
- ISO 27001
- CAIQ
- Trust Center content
- Privacy policy language
- Security documentation
- Approved response narratives
- Internal control evidence

Explain that this reduces one-off work and helps prevent unsupported external claims.

### 4. Show the Stakeholder Review Matrix

Open the Stakeholder Review Matrix.

Explain that not every customer question should be answered immediately by Customer Trust or Sales. Some questions require review from specific stakeholders.

Example routing:
- Legal: contractual language, external commitments, policy interpretation
- Privacy: data handling, DPA, SCCs, retention, deletion, subprocessors
- Security: technical control evidence, encryption, access control, architecture
- Product: product-specific capability claims or roadmap-sensitive questions
- Field Security: enterprise security review and customer-facing technical explanation
- GTM / Customer Success: customer coordination and deal support
- GRC / Customer Trust: evidence mapping, approved narratives, audit-ready documentation

The matrix demonstrates judgment: what can be answered from approved content, what needs review, and what should not be claimed externally without evidence.

### 5. Show unsupported-claim detection

Open a request that includes a risky or unsupported customer-facing claim.

Explain that the workflow flags claims that are not clearly supported by approved evidence or internal controls.

Example:
- Avoid: “We guarantee all customer data remains in-region.”
- Better: “Regional hosting and data handling options are reviewed based on product configuration, contractual commitments, and approved architecture documentation.”

This is designed to support speed without losing control over external representations.

### 6. Show GTM / Customer Success enablement

Open a request that maps to reusable answer-bank content.

Explain that a controlled answer bank helps Customer Success Managers and GTM partners answer common initial questions quickly while routing sensitive or non-standard questions to the right review path.

This mirrors the practical pain point of keeping deal flow moving while avoiding inconsistent or unsupported responses.

## Key Talking Points

- This is a customer trust operating model, not just a dashboard.
- The system helps reduce questionnaire turnaround time through reusable answers, evidence paths, and structured review.
- Regional routing helps anticipate likely customer concerns based on past request patterns.
- The stakeholder review matrix shows judgment and controlled escalation.
- Evidence mapping keeps customer-facing claims tied to approved materials.
- The audit trail supports accountability and repeatability.
